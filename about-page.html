About Traction Stream

50 years on the tools.
Now I want to give something back.

I wasn't a business coach. I wasn't a consultant. I was on site — brush in hand, dust sheets down, pricing jobs, chasing payments, dealing with customers who changed their minds three coats in.

Where it started

My name is Omero Combi. I grew up in Sydney, the son of Italian immigrants who arrived in Australia in 1954. My father and his twin brother were both painters and decorators — trained in the traditional Italian apprenticeship schools — and painting was simply part of family life from the beginning.

I was about ten years old when I was given my first real task: priming iron balustrades on the multi-storey apartments going up across Sydney in the mid-1960s. I can still picture it clearly. Sitting on a wooden crate, Beatles songs in the background, painting away in the sunshine. I've always said that painting was in my blood. It genuinely was.

I was proud to be indentured to my father, and prouder still to receive my first pay as a qualified painter and decorator. After years in the family business I went to Italy in 1981 to explore my heritage, spent two years there, and then headed to London in 1989.

"Despite being told that work was very short in London, I found a day's work footing a ladder. By the afternoon, that painter had injured his ankle. The supervisor looked at me and asked if I could paint properly."

That was the beginning of three extraordinary years with Bulger Wicks, where I eventually supervised around 20 painters across central London and the West End. I eventually became so homesick that I drove to Southampton just to smell the sea air. That was the moment I decided to go home.

Building something real

I returned to Perth, Western Australia, which was booming at the time. My brother joined me and we started as a two-man subcontracting team before building our own firm — eventually employing around 35 painters and completing some of Western Australia's largest infrastructure projects. Hospitals, nursing homes, high-rise apartment buildings, railway station infrastructure.

I served on the board of the Master Painters and Decorators Association of W.A. for seven years, and was appointed as the painting industry representative on the newly created Builders Registration Board for a further three years. I retired in 2015 and moved to Trieste in northeast Italy, near the Slovenian border, to renovate a house I'd bought there. The renovation finished in 2023.

What I noticed over five decades

Most painters and decorators are good at the work. Really good. They can cut in a line freehand, hang paper on a dodgy wall, and make an old ceiling look brand new.

But almost none of them were ever taught how to run the business side properly. Nobody showed them how to price a job so it actually makes money. Nobody explained what happens when you don't put your terms in writing. Nobody warned them that quoting time, travel, and admin eat into their rate — and that being busy doesn't mean the same thing as being profitable.

I know, because I was one of them. I spent years undercharging, absorbing overruns, and hoping the next job would make up the difference. It doesn't. It never does.

"When I finally sat down and worked out what my business actually cost me per hour — the real number, not the one I had in my head — I was shocked. I'd been giving away thousands every year without realising it."

Two painters I knew in particular stayed with me. Both self-taught, both genuinely skilled. One was artistically gifted. But they were frustrated, undercharging, unable to grow. The business knowledge just wasn't there — and there was nowhere obvious to go to get it. That's the person I built Traction Stream for.

What this is

This isn't a coaching programme. There are no webinars. Nobody's going to tell you to level up or smash your goals.

This is practical, trade-specific help for the business side of painting and decorating. Pricing. Quoting. Deposits. Payment terms. Customer control. Protecting your profit. Stopping the awkward conversations before they start. Everything I wish someone had told me in my first year — in plain language, from someone who's actually done the work.

Who this is for

Self-employed painters and decorators — anywhere in the world — who are good at the job but tired of:

  • Guessing their prices
  • Customers messing them about
  • Being busy all year but still tight on money
  • Quoting jobs and never hearing back
  • Finishing work and then chasing payment for weeks
  • Not knowing if their hourly rate actually covers their costs

If that sounds familiar, you're in the right place.

Why listen to me

I don't have a marketing degree. I have 50 years of paint under my fingernails.

I've priced thousands of jobs across three countries. I've dealt with every kind of customer — the good ones, the time-wasters, and the ones who suddenly forgot they agreed to that price. I've run a crew of 35 painters on major infrastructure projects, and I've worked alone on domestic jobs where the customer stood in the doorway telling me how they used to do it themselves.

I've made every mistake a decorator can make on the business side. And I've spent years working out what actually fixes those problems — not in theory, but on real jobs, with real customers, in real houses.

That's what I put into the tools and advice on this site.
